Your Dance App

‘Your Dance App’ was the second project of the Google UX Design certificate where we had to create a website and then its corresponding mobile app. The prompt was: design a responsive website for finding and viewing dance tutorials.

My approach, based on survey responses, was to make this product a single stop for users to find dance tutorials and have them organized by levels, styles, and requirements.
Other features would include the option to upload and share their own videos with other users and get feedback from the dance community.
They'd also be able to add notes, set reminders, and create their own learning paths!

The Problem

According to the research, the platforms most users use to watch dance tutorials are either Youtube, Instagram, or Facebook where they lack the possibility to filter videos or set learning paths. Besides, videos are of all kinds of definitions and the content can also be of poor quality, making the whole experience quite frustrating.

The Goal

To provide a platform that offers a single stop for dance learners to not only watch high quality dance tutorials but also to be able to look them up according to level, style, partner work, or solo choreographies as well as the possibility to follow or create learning paths.
Users also need to be able to set reminders, upload their own videos, and share and connect with the dance community.

Understanding the user

User Research

I conducted user interviews which I then turned into empathy maps.
I discovered that many users struggle to find tutorials online or, when they do, they find it annoying not to have an easy way to save them for later.
They also struggle with the quality of the videos as well as the poor angles of some of them or the lack of a slow-motion feature that would allow them to follow along easily and break down the choreography.

User Story
'As a busy professional who likes to squeeze my free time the most, I want to have access to dance tutorials organized by levels and categories so that I can easily keep up improving on my dance skills even when I can't make it to class or other plans come up at the same time as my dance classes.'
Problem Statement

Amanda is a busy professional trying to juggle hobbies in her limited free time who needs a dance app where she can watch dance tutorials at her own pace and time because she can’t always make it to dance classes.

Starting the design

Information Architecture

Concise AI that makes up for a 'personal organizer' where users can find all the resources they need: paths, videos watched, those saved for later, their notes and reviews, etc.

Paper wireframes

First, I sketched my ideas on paper. I came out with different versions of the same pages and ultimately marked the features which I considered to be essential.

The goal was to make the search as intuitive and easy as possible so users can find what they are looking for as soon as they land on the page.

Digital wireframes

Home page with direct access to the type of dance users are looking for. Search bar to filter by song, singer or instructor if they already know that. From there, they are redirected to the style page where they can sort the videos by levels.

Usability Study

I tested the low-fidelity prototype created out of the first wireframes with a usability study.
